## **1.2 Meaning‑Space as a Manifold**

### **1.2.1 Definition**

Meaning‑space is modeled as a **high‑dimensional manifold** \( M \), where each point represents a configuration of:

- semantic content  
- emotional tone  
- identity posture  
- social context  
- value structure  
- narrative framing  
- attentional stance  

This manifold is:

- continuous  
- deformable  
- context‑sensitive  
- multi‑layered  
- non‑Euclidean  
- locally navigable but globally unchartable  

### **1.2.2 Why a manifold?**

Because:

- meaning is not discrete  
- transitions are smooth until they aren’t  
- folds and hyperfolds behave like topological singularities  
- navigation depends on local curvature, not global maps  
- identity, belief, and narrative form **attractor basins**  

This framing allows us to treat cognitive rigidity, dogma, identity fusion, and doctrinal collapse as **geometric phenomena**, not moral or psychological failures.

## **1.5 Pressure, Curvature, and Capture**

### **1.5.1 Pressure**

Pressure is the **local narrowing of meaning‑space**.

Sources include:

- epistemic pressure (certainty, universality, finality)  
- emotional pressure (fear, shame, awe, belonging)  
- social pressure (conformity, hierarchy, group identity)  
- linguistic pressure (sacred language, binaries, imperatives)  
- narrative pressure (destiny, purity, doom, hero arcs)  

Pressure is not bad.  
Pressure is a **signal**.

### **1.5.2 Curvature**

Curvature is the **change in pressure over time**.

- Positive curvature → narrowing, funneling, capture risk  
- Negative curvature → opening, permeability, optionality  

Curvature is the **shape** of the cognitive trajectory.

### **1.5.3 Capture**

Capture occurs when:

- pressure becomes self‑reinforcing  
- curvature bends toward a single attractor  
- alternatives collapse  
- identity fuses with stance  
- narrative becomes necessary  
- language becomes sacred  
- exit becomes unthinkable  

Capture is a **topological event** — a fold in the manifold.
